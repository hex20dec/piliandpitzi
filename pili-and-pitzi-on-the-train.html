Pili and Pitzi on the Train

Talia V.
February 19, 2023
Updated on February 19, 2023

Pili and Pitzi were on a crowded train one day, heading to visit Pili's grandparents. As they squeezed into their seats, they noticed that there were a lot of people standing, holding onto poles or leaning against the walls. They saw an elderly woman holding onto a pole with a look of exhaustion on her face.

Pili looked at Pitzi and whispered, "She looks like she could use a seat."

Pitzi nodded and looked around. All the other seats were taken, so she stood up and tapped the woman on the arm. "Excuse me," she said, "would you like to sit down? You look tired."

The woman smiled gratefully and sat down, thanking Pitzi for her kindness. Pili and Pitzi felt good about their action, knowing they had helped someone in need.

As they continued their journey, they noticed other people on the train who could benefit from a seat, like a man using crutches. Pili stood up and offered the man her seat. Pili and Pitzi made a mental note to keep an eye out and offer their seats whenever possible.

As the train pulled into the station, Pili and Pitzi made their way to Pili's grandparent's house. Excited to see them, they burst through the front door with big smiles on their faces.

Pili's grandparents greeted them warmly, and the friends eagerly shared their story of how they offered their seats to people on the train. Their grandparents were impressed and proud of their kind actions.

Grandma took Pili and Pitzi aside and talked to them about how important it is to be considerate of others, especially those who may need a little extra help. She shared stories of her own experiences on public transportation and how grateful she was when someone offered her a seat.

Pitzi listened intently, realizing how their small act of kindness had made such a big impact on the elderly couple. He felt a sense of pride knowing that they had made someone's day a little easier and more comfortable.

From that day on, Pili and Pitzi made it a point to always be aware of those around them and offer their seats whenever necessary. They knew that it was just a small gesture, but it could make a huge difference in someone's day. And, every time they did it, they felt a sense of satisfaction and joy knowing that they were doing something good for someone else.
