The Power of Waiting Your Turn: A Story with Pili and Pitzi

Talia V.
February 18, 2023
Updated on February 19, 2023

One day, Pili and Pitzi were playing together in the forest when they heard some commotion nearby. They followed the noise and saw a group of animals gathered around a little pond.

As they approached, they saw that a bird was trying to tell a story, but a few of the other animals were interrupting and talking over her.

Pili and Pitzi could see that the bird was getting upset and frustrated. "Wait your turn, everyone!" said Pili in a loud voice, hoping to be heard over the chatter.

The animals looked at Pili and Pitzi and paused for a moment. "What do you mean, wait our turn?" asked a curious squirrel.

"It means we should let the bird finish her story before we start talking," explained Pitzi.

"Right," added Pili. "It's important to wait your turn before you speak, and not interrupt or talk over someone else. That way, everyone can hear what each of us has to say, and no one feels left out or ignored."

The animals nodded in agreement, and they all sat down to listen to the rest of the bird's story. When she finished, the animals took turns sharing their own stories, and everyone felt happy and included.

As Pili and Pitzi walked back home, they felt proud of themselves for standing up for the bird and for teaching their friends about waiting their turn to speak.
